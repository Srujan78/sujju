About Saalumarada Thimmakka

Saalumarada Thimmakka, also known as Aalada Marada Timakka, is a renowned environmentalist from Karnataka, India. She gained fame for her incredible contribution to afforestation, having planted and nurtured over 385 banyan trees along a 4 km stretch between Hulikal and Kudur. Her work in conserving nature and protecting the environment has received national and international recognition.

Her Early Life and Inspiration

Born in a rural village, Thimmakka worked as a laborer and could not have children. She and her husband Chikkaiah decided to plant trees as their legacy to the world. They began planting banyan saplings, watering and caring for them daily. Over the years, their dedication turned barren lands into lush greenery.

Awards and Recognition

Saalumarada Thimmakka has received numerous awards, including:

  • Padma Shri (2019)
  • National Citizen's Award
  • Indira Priyadarshini Vrikshamitra Award
  • Karnataka Rajyotsava Award

Her dedication to the environment has inspired many to take part in tree plantation drives and environmental conservation efforts across India and beyond.
